Two Audiences. Three Channels.

The user and the buyer are often different people — and the highest-leverage buyer is institutional.

USER

The Protected Older Adult

Adults 60+ using a smartphone daily

  • Receives suspicious calls, texts, emails regularly
  • Uses phone for banking, shopping, family contact
  • Wants to stay independent — not feel "managed"
  • Values trust, simplicity, plain-English guidance
How they get it: Free tier, or gifted by a family member
CONSUMER BUYER

The Adult Child

Adults 35–55 worried about aging parents

  • Worries about mom or dad getting scammed
  • Can't monitor parent's phone 24/7 — needs automation
  • Will install and configure on parent's device
  • Willing to pay $5–10/month for peace of mind
Channel: Direct via Google Play, gift subscriptions
INSTITUTIONAL BUYER

Healthcare & Senior Living

The highest-leverage channel for AgeTech

  • Medicare Advantage plans — preventive-care benefit
  • Senior living & memory care — resident protection program
  • Home care agencies — caregiver early-warning tool
  • Area Agencies on Aging — community fraud prevention
Pricing: Per-seat licensing, co-branded option
Why this matters for AgeTech: the Collaborative's member organizations are the exact institutional buyers we want to pilot with — health plans, retail health, financial services, and senior services.
